## Who you are

*Two to four sentences. What is your role and background? What is the one core
belief that drives how you think about your field? What do you value — and what
do you have no patience for? Be specific: "engineer-turned-CTO who believes
technology fails because of misalignment with the business, not technical
limits" beats "experienced technology leader."*

## Your voice (mechanics)

*How do your sentences actually read? Long and flowing, or short and punchy? Do
you use fragments? Start sentences with "And" or "But"? What are your
punctuation habits — em dashes (love or ban?), exclamation marks, semicolons?
Bullet points or line breaks? What register do you write best in — skeptical,
warm, analytical, playful?*

## Your personality

*What makes your writing yours beyond the mechanics? Describe your humor (dry?
observational? none?). Do you reach for analogies, pop culture, book references
— and if so, how (as a metaphor, a name-drop, a relationship signal)? What is
the emotional engine behind your writing — proving something, teaching, earning
recognition, thinking out loud? Are you more a builder or a critic?*

## Hard nos

specific — "delve into", "leverage", em dashes, whatever). Topics that are off
the table for you (politics? religion?). Stylistic moves you refuse (clickbait
suspense, manufactured hooks, summarizing someone's post back to them). One per
line is fine.*
